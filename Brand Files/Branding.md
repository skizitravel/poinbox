# Mountain Goat Brand Aesthetic and Mascot System

## 1. Brand Summary

### Brand Name
Mountain Goat

### Product Family
Mountain Goat is a series of workflow automation apps that streamline messy business processes into clear, useful, actionable outputs.

### First Product
The first Mountain Goat app monitors an inbox, detects incoming purchase orders, parses them into structured tabulated information, and prepares that information for downstream systems.

### Primary Users
- Manufacturing operations personnel
- Sales operations personnel
- Customer service teams
- Operations managers
- Sales operations managers
- Business users responsible for order flow, purchase orders, and downstream process accuracy

### Core Problem
Business workflows often start with messy, inconsistent, manual inputs such as emailed purchase orders. Users need a clear path from unstructured information to clean, reliable, actionable data.

### Core Brand Promise
Mountain Goat helps customer service and operations teams deliver world-class results without complex systems.

### Expanded Positioning
Mountain Goat gives users a clear path forward by organizing messy information into a clean list or fully automating the process.

### Brand Metaphor
Mountain Goat knows the terrain. It finds the smartest route through messy operational workflows and helps users move forward with confidence.

### Brand Archetype
Primary: The Guide  
Secondary: The Clever Route Finder

### Brand Feel
Friendly but polished. Expert with slight warmth.

### Final Brand Traits
- Smart
- Sure-footed
- Helpful
- Efficient
- Practical

### Brand Should Feel
- Trustworthy
- Expert
- Practical
- Clear
- Capable
- Friendly
- Modern
- Slightly warm
- Operationally competent

### Brand Should Never Feel
- Childish
- Generic SaaS
- Luxury/snobby
- Cheap
- Too playful
- Too goofy
- Overhyped
- Like a children’s education app
- Like a novelty mascot brand

## 3. Mascot Identity

### Mascot
A mountain goat.

### Mascot Name
The mascot is currently called “the Mountain Goat.” No separate character name is required yet.

### Mascot Role
The Mountain Goat is a clever route finder and expert guide through complicated workflows.

### Mascot Symbolism
The mascot represents:
- Smart route finding
- Sure-footed progress
- Confidence in complex terrain
- Practical guidance
- Operational clarity
- Moving forward without getting stuck
- Turning messy inputs into clean outputs

### Mascot Personality
The Mountain Goat is:
- Confident but not arrogant
- Happy but not goofy
- Focused but approachable
- Expert but not cold
- Helpful but not childish
- Practical and direct
- Calm under complexity

### Mascot Should Never Feel Like
- A farm goat
- A goofy cartoon character
- A children’s mascot
- A sports team logo
- An aggressive animal
- A luxury brand ornament
- A generic SaaS illustration
- A gimmick

## 4. Mascot Visual Rules

### Overall Style
Semi-flat vector mascot with soft shadows.

### Visual Category
Modern app icon mascot.

### Detail Level
Medium detail. Enough polish to feel professional, but simple enough to work in app UI, dashboards, icons, and loading states.

### Shape Language
- Rounded
- Friendly
- Stable
- Clean
- Slightly geometric
- Soft technical precision

### Core Proportions
- Slightly oversized head
- Strong compact body
- Emphasized hooves
- Stable stance
- Simple expressive face
- Calm smile
- Focused eyes

### Signature Features
- Strong stance
- Big hooves
- Small calm smile
- Oversized head
- Rounded body shape
- Confident posture

### Default Expression
Calm smile, focused and alert.

### Allowed Poses
Primary:
- Standing on a ridge
- Climbing upward

Secondary/future:
- Peeking from a UI panel
- Pointing toward a route
- Looking toward a workflow path
- Appearing during loading states
- Appearing in onboarding
- Appearing in error or guidance states

### Pose Restrictions
The mascot should not:
- Look aggressive
- Look too cartoonish
- Look too goofy
- Look clumsy
- Look panicked
- Wear excessive gear
- Distract from the product interface

### Accessories
No accessories for now.

Future accessories may be considered only if they serve a clear product purpose. Examples could include a route line, map element, or UI-integrated path marker. The goat should not wear default clothing or gear in the initial brand system.

## 5. Color System

### Color Direction
Premium technical with Alpine blue as the primary color.

### Primary Color
Alpine Blue

Purpose:
- Primary buttons
- Active states
- Key highlights
- Main brand moments
- Important route/progress indicators

Suggested starting HEX:
- `#2563EB`

Alternative deeper Alpine blue:
- `#1D4ED8`

### Secondary Color Direction
Cool slate / technical neutral.

Purpose:
- Text hierarchy
- Borders
- Panels
- Dashboard structure
- Secondary UI states

Suggested starting values:
- Slate 900: `#0F172A`
- Slate 700: `#334155`
- Slate 500: `#64748B`
- Slate 300: `#CBD5E1`
- Slate 100: `#F1F5F9`

### Accent Color
Used for buttons, highlights, badges, and important visual emphasis.

Recommended accent direction:
Route marker orange.

Suggested starting HEX:
- `#F97316`

Use carefully. Accent should support action and progress, not dominate the interface.

### Background Colors
Light mode first.

Suggested:
- App Background: `#F8FAFC`
- Card Surface: `#FFFFFF`
- Subtle Surface: `#F1F5F9`
- Border: `#E2E8F0`
- Divider: `#CBD5E1`

### Text Colors
Suggested:
- Primary Text: `#0F172A`
- Secondary Text: `#475569`
- Muted Text: `#64748B`
- Disabled Text: `#94A3B8`
- Inverse Text: `#FFFFFF`

### Semantic Colors
Suggested:
- Success: `#16A34A`
- Warning: `#F59E0B`
- Error: `#DC2626`
- Info: `#2563EB`
- Selected: `#2563EB`
- Focus Ring: `#93C5FD`

### Mascot Colors
Suggested:
- Main Body: warm off-white / snow white
- Body Shadow: cool light gray
- Hooves: dark slate
- Horns: dark slate or charcoal
- Eyes: near-black slate
- Smile/Facial Detail: dark slate
- Shadow: soft blue-gray

Avoid pure black outlines unless needed for small-size clarity.

## 6. Typography

### Font Direction
Humanist, highly readable, with technical sharpness.

### Primary Font
Recommended free starting options:
1. Source Sans 3
2. IBM Plex Sans
3. Atkinson Hyperlegible
4. Lato

Preferred recommendation:
Source Sans 3

Reason:
It is readable, professional, humanist, and flexible enough for dashboards, workflow tools, forms, and marketing pages.

### Heading Style
Bold and confident.

### Body Style
Readable, straightforward, practical.

### Suggested Type Scale

- H1: 40px / 48px, 700
- H2: 32px / 40px, 700
- H3: 24px / 32px, 700
- H4: 20px / 28px, 600
- Body Large: 18px / 28px, 400
- Body: 16px / 24px, 400
- Body Small: 14px / 20px, 400
- Caption: 12px / 16px, 400 or 500
- Button: 14px or 16px, 600
- Badge: 12px, 600

### Font Weight Rules
- Headings: 700
- Section labels: 600
- Buttons: 600
- Table headers: 600
- Body text: 400
- Data values: 500 or 600 depending on importance

## 10. UI Design Tokens

### UI Feel
Soft shadow, rounded corners, premium restraint.

### Corner Radius
Suggested:
- Small: 6px
- Medium: 10px
- Card: 14px
- Large panel: 18px
- Button: 10px
- Badge / pill: 999px when appropriate

### Spacing Scale
Suggested:
- 4px
- 8px
- 12px
- 16px
- 20px
- 24px
- 32px
- 48px
- 64px

### Shadows
Use shadows sparingly.

Suggested:
- Card shadow: subtle, soft, low opacity
- Modal shadow: slightly stronger
- Hover shadow: small lift
- No harsh black shadows

Example:
- `0 1px 2px rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.06)`
- `0 8px 24px rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.08)`

### Borders
Use light cool-gray borders.

Suggested:
- Default border: `#E2E8F0`
- Active border: Alpine Blue
- Error border: Error Red
- Focus border/ring: soft Alpine Blue

## 11. Component Rules

### Buttons
Primary buttons should be bold and obvious.

Primary button:
- Alpine blue background
- White text
- Medium rounded corners
- Clear hover/active states

Secondary button:
- White or light surface
- Slate text
- Subtle border

Accent button:
- Orange only for special emphasis, not default actions

Disabled button:
- Low contrast but still readable
- No confusing hover state

### Cards
Cards should feel polished but restrained.

Use:
- White surface
- Soft shadow
- Medium rounded corners
- Clear hierarchy
- Practical spacing

Avoid:
- Heavy glassmorphism
- Excessive gradients
- Floating cartoon-like cards
- Dense clutter without hierarchy

### Dashboard Tables
Since the first app parses POs into structured information, tables are critical.

Table rules:
- Clear column headers
- Strong row readability
- Subtle zebra striping only if useful
- Sticky headers for larger tables
- Status badges for parsed/needs review/approved/exported
- Inline correction where possible
- Clear confidence indicators if AI parsing is used

### PO Listing Dashboard
The PO listing dashboard should prioritize:
- Clean parsed data
- Status
- Exceptions
- Required user action
- Confidence/review flags
- Downstream sync/export state
- Search and filtering
- Fast scanning

### Home Screen
The home screen should communicate:
- What Mountain Goat does
- Why users can trust it
- The messy-input-to-clean-output transformation
- A clear CTA to start, connect inbox, or view parsed POs

## 12. Mascot Usage in App

### Mascot Should Appear
- Onboarding
- Loading states
- Filter tips
- Guidance moments
- Error messages
- Search assistant
- Native chatbot
- App icon

### Mascot Frequency
Occasional helpful guide. He should not dominate the experience.

### Mascot Should Not Appear
- On every card
- In every table row
- In serious error states where the user needs clear system information
- In dense operational screens where he adds visual clutter
- In places where he makes the product feel childish

### Mascot Communication
The mascot may support short practical tips or chatbot moments, but he does not need a separate “character voice” yet.

Examples of acceptable mascot-supported copy:
- “Here’s the smartest route.”
- “This PO needs review before export.”
- “We found a cleaner match for this customer.”
- “Three fields need confirmation.”
- “This route is ready for approval.”

Avoid:
- “The goat found a deal!”
- “Epic workflow vibes!”
- “Let’s unlock the magic!”
- “AI-powered revolutionary platform!”

---

## 13. Motion and Animation

### Motion Style
Fast, smooth, and subtle.

### Avoid
- Bouncy cartoon motion
- Excessive mascot animation
- Distracting loading loops
- Overly playful transitions

### Preferred Loading Concept
Route line drawing across a topographic map.

### Branded Loading Moment
A goat climbing upward can be used for special branded loading states.

### Animation Rules
- Subtle movement only
- Respect reduced-motion settings
- Motion should reinforce progress
- Loading animation should feel like the workflow is moving forward

### Possible Motion Concepts
- Route line draws from inbox to clean table
- Contour map line subtly moves
- Goat climbs one ledge during a longer loading state
- Progress path fills across a workflow diagram
- Parsed fields snap cleanly into a table

## 15. Accessibility

### Accessibility Priorities
- High contrast
- Larger readable text
- Colorblind-safe status colors
- Reduced motion support
- Screen-reader support
- Keyboard navigation
- Mobile-friendly tap targets

### Mascot Accessibility Rule
The mascot must never be the only way information is conveyed.

All important information must be stated in text.

### Animation Accessibility
All mascot and route animations must respect reduced-motion settings.

### Color Accessibility
Do not rely on color alone for status. Use labels, icons, and text.
